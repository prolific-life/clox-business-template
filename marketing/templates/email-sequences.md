# Outbound email sequences (B2B)

Skeletons for `compose-outbound-email`. Hard rules: ≤120 words, one
CTA, personalization from the lead's research note (never mail-merge
mush), 3 touches max, 3–4 days apart, STOP on any reply. All sends
are approval-gated (contract invariant #3).

## Touch 1 — the trigger intro
- Subject: their context, not us ("<their initiative> + <pain>")
- Line 1: why them, why now (the trigger from the research note)
- Line 2–3: the wedge — what we do for <their segment> in their words
- CTA: one low-friction ask (15 min, or "worth a look?")

## Touch 2 — the value drop (3–4 days later)
- No "just bumping". Bring something: a relevant result, a 2-line
  teardown of their funnel/site, a resource from our content
- CTA: same ask, softer

## Touch 3 — the breakup (3–4 days later)
- One line: closing the loop, door stays open
- Optional: link the single best proof asset

## After
- Reply → human-stage: relay to the user via feedback card if a
  decision/call is needed; never auto-respond past scheduling
  logistics.
- No reply after touch 3 → stage `lost (no response)` in leads.md;
  eligible for a NEW sequence only if a NEW trigger appears.
