# Writing style guide

> Mechanical writing rules an AI can follow verbatim. Generated and
> refreshed by the `build-style-guide` skill from REAL samples (shipped
> posts, site copy, the founder's writing). Until first generation,
> the defaults below apply. Voice/personality lives in
> `docs/branding/identity.md` — this file is the MECHANICS.

## Sentence structure
- Mix: ~60% medium (15–25 words, explanatory), ~30% short (5–12,
  emphasis/transitions), ~10% long (25+, only for genuinely complex
  ideas).
- Lead sentences carry the point; never bury the claim after a comma
  stack.

## Vocabulary
- Plain words over jargon; name the product feature, not the category
  buzzword.
- Banned until proven in samples: "revolutionize", "unleash",
  "game-changer", "10x" (unless literal), emoji walls.

## Tone & techniques
- Confident, specific, generous — show the thing, give the number,
  share the how.
- Rhetorical questions: max one per piece. Alliteration/wordplay only
  where it reads natural.

## Per-channel deltas
- **X:** punchy, first line is the hook, no hashtags unless trending
  and relevant.
- **LinkedIn:** story or lesson shape, short paragraphs, no corporate
  filler.
- **Reddit:** value-first, zero sell in the first 80%, disclose
  affiliation.
- **Email:** subject ≤ 50 chars, one CTA, skimmable in 20 seconds.
